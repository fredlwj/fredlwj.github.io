The curious case of one-dollar CEO salaries: Evidence from market reaction to salary decision announcements

  • This essay examines the market reaction to the announcement of a $1 CEO salary decision and teases out the information in the announcement, using both explicit reasons for the decision and mechanisms for dealing with the base salary in CEO compensation packages.

  • Presented at The 94th International Atlantic Economic Society Conference, October 2022 and The 62nd Southwestern Finance Association Annual Conference, March 2023

  • Coauthored with Anna N. Danielova

  • Paper submitted to International Journal of Managerial Finance

The value of corporate social responsibility: Evidence from market reaction to data breach announcements

  • This essay investigates the role of corporate social responsibility activities engaged in by public firms in the event of a data breach. Two CSR ratings are employed to differentiate the breached firms for examining the difference in the announcement-date stock abnormal returns between high and low CSR groups.

  • Presented at The 96th International Atlantic Economic Society Conference, October 2023 and The 63rd Southwestern Finance Association Annual Conference, February 2024

  • Coauthored with Trevor W. Chamberlain and Rahman Khokhar

  • Research note submitted to International Advances in Economic Research

Corporate operating performance following seasoned equity offerings: It pays to pay dividends

  • This essay investigates the connection between dividend payouts and investments of seasoned equity offering (SEO) firms, analyzing how dividend payouts relate to post-issue investment and operating performance in terms of overinvestment.

  • Presented at The 92nd International Atlantic Economic Society Conference, October 2021 and The 61st Southwestern Finance Association Annual Conference, March 2022
